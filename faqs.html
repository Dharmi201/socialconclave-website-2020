FAQ's

Frequently asked Questions

What is the social aspect of Social Conclave?

Participants gather to discuss a number of pre-defined social issues affecting the nation, and exhibit their sensitivity towards the same by finding a multitude of solutions for these issues.

How is this different from a MUN?

1. Unlike conventional MUNs, Social Conclave promotes discussion and not debate.
2.Every participant will get a certificate from the United Nations.
3.We have a Field Visit on the second day wherein the participants will get an opportunity to touch the lives of the vulnerable groups and uplift their lives, followed by a Mock Panchayat which will be conducted like a regular panchayat with 5 pradhans overlooking the discussions, here the delegates will get an opportunity to showcase their debating prowess.
4.The most feasible solution will be implemented by the Social Impact Team of our college.
5.Format and procedure isn’t as strict as a MUN, since Social Conclave is a socially inclined conference.

How difficult is Social Conclave?

Social Conclave is a place for discussion and not debate. The agendas might seem tough but through multiple panel discussions and field visit, delegates will be able to deep dive into the subject and present solutions which would have the capability to change the face of India for good.

Do I need to be a good debater to be a part of this conference?

You will be judged on the feasibility of your solution and the points you put forward during the mock panchayat and not on how well of a speaker you are. Social Conclave isn’t and won’t ever be a debate. It’s a place of discussion.

What if I don’t have a team for the conference?

In case you are an individual participant with no team, it is on us to provide you with a team based on your chosen or allotted agenda.

Is it compulsory to attend all the three days of the conference?

Only those participants who will be present on all the 3 days of the conference will be awarded with certificates signed by the UN.

How prestigious are the certificates?

Since they are signed by the UN, they hold a lot of importance and thus will add prestige to your CV and it will work wonders for anyone who’s looking to pursue higher education abroad.

What will happen in the field visit?

You will get an opportunity to visit the most vulnerable groups and get to understand their problems. You will get a chance to interact one-on-one with the downtrodden.

Where will the field visit be?

It will be within 100KM radius from the venue and travel for the same will be arranged by us.

What is a mock panchayat?

Mock Panchayat will be taking place on the second day and in simple words it will provide the delegates an opportunity to showcase how opinionated and sound are they. To ensure fair competition, the topics will be of dynamic in nature.

How long will the presentation have to be?

The presentation will have to be at least 10 minutes long and the delegates can present in any form they want be it a PowerPoint presentation or a skit or a report, whatever is feasible for both the delegates and the judges.

What is the judging criterion?

You will be judged on the basis of your performance in the Mock Panchayat and the feasibility and the uniqueness of your solution.

Can I be a part of the implementation?

Yes, you can. We will be contacting you once we start the implementation and you can join us according to your convenience.

What all does the registration fees include?

The registration fees will include 3 meals for 3 days, full access to the comedy night and the social mixer. It will also include the travelling expenses incurred during the field visit. It will basically include everything a delegate would be needing during the span of the conference.

What about travel?

Travel expenses incurred during the travel to the site of field visit is included.

Does accommodation include food?

Breakfast, Lunch and High Tea will be provided by us during the span of the conference, the rest of the meals are upon you. You get good food for very cheap prices at our college canteen, which is open for all.

What is the dress code?

Delegates are expected to be dressed in Western formals on the 1st and 3rd day of the conference and Indian traditional on the 2nd day of the conference.

Cancellation Policy

The conference does not entertain any cancellations once the registration and payment procedure is done. The registration is done only post the finalisation from the delegate.
